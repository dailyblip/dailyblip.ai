20 Suno Prompts for Better Song Structure, Bigger Choruses, and Stronger Hooks

Fix weak arrangements and forgettable choruses with these structure-focused prompts.

Last reviewed July 20, 2026
AI MusicTools & Workflow

A song can have a great melody and still fall flat if the structure doesn't build anywhere. These prompts target the arrangement itself, not just the sound.

Prompts to try

Twenty prompts, each aimed at a different structural problem — pick the one that matches what's actually wrong with your song.

PROMPTS TO TRY
Bigger chorus via production contrast
Verse stripped back, chorus full band, wide stereo, anthemic
Real dynamic build into the chorus
Quiet intimate verse building to explosive chorus, dynamic contrast, cinematic swell
Repeated, memorable hook line
[Hook] repeated hook line, belted and powerful delivery, high energy
Bridge that actually contrasts
Bridge strips down to vocals and single instrument before final chorus returns full
Second verse that doesn't repeat the first
Second verse adds one new instrument layer not present in verse one, subtle build
Automatic pre-chorus lift
[Pre-Chorus] rising energy, building tension, drums entering gradually into the drop
Cold open with an instrumental hook
[Intro] instrumental hook, melodic riff introduced before vocals enter, memorable motif
Call-and-response chorus
[Chorus] call and response between lead vocal and backing vocals, layered group energy
Key change for the final chorus
Final chorus modulates up a key, bigger arrangement, more intensity than previous choruses
Breakdown before the last hook
[Break] instrumental breakdown, strips to rhythm section only, tension building back to final hook
Ad-libs to keep a repeated chorus interesting
[Chorus] main vocal line with ad-libs and vocal runs layered on the repeat, not identical to first chorus
Stronger outro that doesn't just fade
[Outro] hook line repeats and strips down instrument by instrument to a final solo vocal or instrument
Pre-chorus that sets up the hook lyrically
[Pre-Chorus] lyrics build anticipation directly toward the hook line that follows
Bigger drums for the final chorus only
Final chorus adds double-time drum pattern and extra percussion layer not present earlier
Instrumental hook that recurs
Signature instrumental riff from the intro reappears between chorus and final chorus as a recurring motif
Vocal harmony stacking for impact
[Chorus] three-part vocal harmony stack on the hook line, wide and full
Verse that's deliberately restrained
Verse: sparse arrangement, single instrument and vocal only, deliberate restraint to make the chorus land harder
A bridge that changes the groove entirely
Bridge shifts to half-time groove, different rhythmic feel than the rest of the song, before returning to full tempo
Tag ending on the hook
[Outro] repeats final hook line three times, each time with less instrumentation, ending on a cappella vocal
Building energy across three choruses
Each chorus repeat adds one new layer of instrumentation, first chorus sparsest, final chorus fullest

What actually controls structure

Structure tags like [Verse], [Pre-Chorus], and [Chorus] in your lyrics field do more for arrangement than any style descriptor.

Suno treats a [Pre-Chorus] as a transition and builds energy into it automatically, which is an easy way to get a lift you didn't have to describe yourself.

If a chorus still isn't landing, the fix is usually in the lyrics field, not the style field. Tighten the actual hook line before you touch the production tags.

Structure problems rarely get fixed by better sound. They get fixed by better contrast between sections, and Suno responds to that contrast if you actually describe it.

KEY TAKEAWAYS
  • Use [Pre-Chorus] tags to get an automatic energy build into your chorus.
  • Describe production contrast directly: "verse stripped back, chorus full band."
  • If a hook isn't landing, fix the line itself before touching production tags.
