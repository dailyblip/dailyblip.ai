How to Use AI to Brainstorm Better Song Lyrics Without Sounding Generic

Use AI as a brainstorming partner for themes and imagery, not a lyric-writing machine.

Last reviewed July 20, 2026
WritingAI Music

Ask an AI to "write a song about heartbreak" and you'll get something that sounds like every other AI song about heartbreak. The fix is asking better, narrower questions.

Ask for pieces, not a finished lyric

Instead of "write a chorus about missing someone," try "give me ten specific images of an empty apartment after someone leaves."

Ask for a list of concrete objects tied to your theme, not abstract feelings. Specific objects are what make a lyric feel real.

Request multiple rhyme options for one line instead of a whole verse. You'll pick the one that actually sounds like you.

Use it to rewrite, not originate

Write your own rough draft first, even a bad one. Then ask AI to suggest tighter phrasing for specific lines you're stuck on.

Ask it to point out which lines are the most generic in a draft you already wrote. That's often more useful than asking it to generate new ones.

If a suggested line is good, change at least one word before you use it. That small edit is usually the difference between borrowed and yours.

The generic-AI-lyric problem isn't really an AI problem. It's a vague-prompt problem. Ask for something specific and you'll get something specific back.

PROMPTS TO TRY
Ask for images, not feelings
Give me ten specific, physical images that represent losing touch with someone, without naming the emotion directly.
Ask for rhyme options on one line
Give me five different natural-sounding ways to end this line with a rhyme, not a forced one: [paste your line]
Ask AI to flag your own generic lines
Read this draft and point out which specific lines feel the most generic or overused, and why: [paste your draft]
KEY TAKEAWAYS
  • Ask for specific images and objects, not abstract feelings.
  • Write a rough draft yourself before asking AI to help rewrite it.
  • Always change at least one word in any line AI suggests.
