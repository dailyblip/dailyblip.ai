The brief

1. The Anthropic $1.5B book copyright settlement is approved — ~$3,000 per allegedly pirated title — setting the baseline for what AI training data licensing looks like going forward.

3. Google shipped three new Gemini models today and confirmed Gemini 4 is already in training — a faster-than-expected roadmap signal for creators building on the platform.
4. Substack has deployed AI-detection scanning across all posts and comments via Pangram — writers publishing AI-assisted work there should understand how it flags content.
5. Meshy launched a conversational 3D Agent for iterative asset creation, letting artists refine models through dialogue while maintaining style consistency across pipeline exports.
6. Spotify, Netflix, YouTube, and TikTok are converging into AI-curated universal apps — format boundaries are dissolving as competition shifts entirely to time-on-platform.
